Headache

Headaches may originate from the neck, muscle tension or the jaw. An integrated approach helps reduce both intensity and frequency.

A headache is head pain that can be occasional or recurrent. It is often linked to muscle tension, tissue sensitivity, or overload in the neck and upper back.

Common triggers include stress, teeth clenching, sustained desk posture, limited cervical mobility, and excessive load on the upper trapezius and suboccipital region.

When left unaddressed, headaches can become more frequent, interfere with sleep and concentration, and create a cycle of tension that feeds the pain. They may also be associated with neck stiffness and jaw discomfort.

At Kinésica we combine hands-on care and postural re-education. Depending on your presentation, we integrate osteopathy, TMJ work, gentle manual manipulations and RPG to release tension and improve control of the neck and shoulder girdle.

Your assessment includes questions about habits, sleep and stress, plus mobility tests for the neck and jaw. Each session sets clear goals, self-care guidance and simple exercises to help maintain progress.

Related complications

  • Neck stiffness
  • Jaw pain and clenching
  • Associated shoulder pain
  • Sleep disruption
  • Increasing episode frequency
