Integrated Approaches to Treat Cervical Pain

Mechanical neck pain is a common musculoskeletal condition characterized by pain in the cervical region without a serious structural cause.

Its origin is often related to postural imbalances, joint and muscular dysfunctions, and movement restrictions. In this context, manual therapy offers a set of effective tools to address these dysfunctions in an individualized and holistic way.

Mechanical Low Back Pain: An Integrated Manual Therapy Approach

Mechanical low back pain is one of the most common reasons for medical consultation and work-related disability worldwide. It is characterized by localized pain in the lumbar region, of non-inflammatory and non-infectious origin, and is often associated with postural alterations, biomechanical overload, joint dysfunctions, and/or muscular imbalances.
